❯ Why does the line "1010 FOR AX%=1 TO NA%" get the error "?SYNTAX ERROR IN     
  1010" in a Commodore 64 emulator?                                             

● C64 BASIC V2 does not support the % integer type suffix. That's a Microsoft
  BASIC feature (GW-BASIC, Applesoft, QBasic), but Commodore 64 BASIC V2 has no 
  integer variable type — all numeric variables are floating point, and % is
  simply not valid as part of a variable name.

  The parser hits AX% and chokes on the % character.

  Fix: Remove all % suffixes from variable names throughout your program. AX%
  becomes AX, NA% becomes NA, RV% becomes RV, etc.

  One caveat: C64 BASIC only distinguishes the first two characters of a
  variable name, so CA and CAR are the same variable. Keep that in mind when
  shortening names from the subroutine map in your CLAUDE.md (e.g. CC% and CD%
  would clash with CC and CD if you also have variables starting with those
  pairs).
